# Citation Styles Reference

APA7 is the default style for all PRIMA Scholar output. Other styles can be requested by the user.

## Style Comparison

| Feature | APA7 | Harvard | Chicago (Author-Date) | MLA | Vancouver |
|---------|------|---------|----------------------|-----|-----------|
| In-text format | (Author, Year) | (Author Year) | (Author Year) | (Author Page) | Numbered [1] |
| Multiple authors | et al. at 3+ | et al. at 3+ | et al. at 4+ | et al. at 3+ | et al. at 7+ |
| Reference list name | References | Reference List | Bibliography | Works Cited | References |
| Title case | Sentence case (articles) | Sentence case | Headline case | Title case | Sentence case |
| DOI format | https://doi.org/xxx | https://doi.org/xxx | https://doi.org/xxx | Not standard | DOI: xxx |
| Common in | Psychology, education, social sciences | UK universities, business | History, humanities | Literature, arts | Medicine, nursing, sciences |

## When to Use Each

| Style | Typical Context |
|-------|----------------|
| **APA7** | Default. Psychology, education, organisational behaviour, social sciences. Most academic journals in psychology, education, organisational behaviour, and social sciences. |
| **Harvard** | UK university assignments, some business schools. Very similar to APA7 in practice — main difference is no comma before year in-text. |
| **Chicago** | Humanities, history. Author-date variant is similar to APA; notes-bibliography variant uses footnotes. |
| **MLA** | Literature, arts, cultural studies. Page numbers in-text rather than year. |
| **Vancouver** | Biomedical, clinical, nursing. Numbered references in order of appearance. |

## Key Differences from APA7

### Harvard

- No comma between author and year: (Smith 2020) not (Smith, 2020)
- Otherwise nearly identical to APA7

### Chicago (Author-Date)

- et al. threshold is 4 authors (APA7 is 3)
- Bibliography uses headline capitalisation for titles
- Otherwise similar to APA7

### MLA

- No year in-text — uses page numbers: (Smith 45)
- Works Cited list uses title case
- Medium-specific formatting (e.g., "Web", "Print" in older editions)

### Vancouver

- Numbered superscript or bracketed references in order of first appearance
- Reference list is numbered, not alphabetical
- Completely different workflow — sources numbered sequentially as they appear

## Implementation Note

When a user requests a non-APA7 style, adapt the in-text citation format and reference list formatting accordingly. The research workflow, source selection, and synthesis quality remain identical regardless of citation style.
